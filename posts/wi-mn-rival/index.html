Wisconsin Really is Better Than Minnesota

Author

Lily Hemmersbach

Published

December 5, 2025

The border battle is basically a personality trait at this point for Wisconsin and Minnesota. If there is one thing mid-westerners love more than cheese curds and football is this rivalry. Wisconsin has cheese curds, tailgating, and beer. What does Minnesota even have?

sb <- "https://www.pro-football-reference.com/super-bowl/" 

sbchamp <- sb |>
  read_html() |>
   html_node(xpath = '//*[@id="super_bowls"]') |>
html_table() 
  
appearances <- sbchamp |>
     pivot_longer(cols = c(Winner, Loser), values_to = "team") |>
  count(team, name = "appearances")

winsonly <- sbchamp |>
  select(Winner) |>
   count(Winner, name = "wins") |>
  arrange(desc(wins))

loseronly <- sbchamp |>
  select(Loser) |>
   count(Loser, name = "loser") |>
  arrange(desc(loser))

GB <- winsonly |>
  filter(Winner == "Green Bay Packers")


offdef <- read_csv("https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/e/2PACX-1vQ2q17BqWtzbtCjTtHVKDXkwN8wkD2iXww5pSWUZVBfgJkB5Oomelb5DeFyq_SzSP6TyTnG9hV41h_S/pub?gid=0&single=true&output=csv") |>
  group_by(Team) |>
  summarize(
    avg_ORtg = mean(ORtg, na.rm = TRUE), avg_DRtg = mean(DRtg, na.rm = TRUE)
  ) |>
  arrange(desc(avg_ORtg))

Two key metrics, offensive and defensive rating, provide insight into how efficiently teams score points and how well they prevent opponents from scoring. This chart visualizes the average offensive and defensive percentage over recent seasons. The blue points represent offensive ratings, while the red points represent defensive ratings. Each line connects a team’s offensive and defensive ratings, allowing a quick visual comparison of strengths and weaknesses.

This chart visualizes total Super Bowl wins across NFL franchises, and the contrast between these two NFC North rivals is striking. The Packers’ bar stands filled in dark green, symbolizing the legacy they’ve built over decades. Meanwhile, the Vikings are absent from this highlighted list of champions. Four appearances, four losses, and decades without a return have left Minnesota watching from the outside as their biggest rival continues to build on a winning tradition.

Don’t get me wrong, the Vikings have made it to the Super bowl four times, but have choked every single time. Close calls? A few. Championships? None.

Even after this first two graphs show the real winner of the battle with the lack of Minnesota even pulling off one Super Bowl win, you’re still not convinced? With this last graph, it is the win percentage in the MLB. Take a look at who comes out on top more.

Across all the graphs, one thing is clear, Wisconsin is the real winner of this border battle and will more then likely remain on top when it comes to sports, as well as beer and cheese. Momentum matters in rivalries, and Wisconsin has it. Right now the border battle isn’t just a rivalry, it’s a reminder on how Wisconsin is setting the standards between the two states.
